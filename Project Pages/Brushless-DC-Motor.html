Brushless DC Motor

Workshop Tings

Overview

This project involved the assembly of a small brushless DC motor using a combination of laser cutting, 3D printing, PCB soldering, and metalwork. The focus was on hands-on workshop skills, proper use of tools, and precision.

Tools and Fabrication Processes

Laser Cutting

  • Prepared a laser cutting file in Inkscape with red lines for cutting and black lines for engraving
  • Ensured all lines were continuous and properly formatted for the laser cutter
  • Loaded the file into the laser cutting software to cut and engrave parts of the motor housing

3D Printing

  • Arranged provided CAD models in a slicing program to select printer type, filament, and orientation
  • Printed components were cleaned and excess material was removed before assembly

PCB Soldering and Wiring

  • Soldered components onto the motor control PCB
  • Created wires for connections including switches and a 6V battery pack
  • Used wire stripping, crimping into Molex headers, and heat shrink tubing to ensure secure connections

Metal Workshop

  • Marked out the metal base by marking outlines with a height gauge and etching tool
  • Used a press to cut and bend metal parts for the motor base
  • Drilled holes for assembly using a drill press
  • Made the motors electromagnet using a drill to wind thin copper wire around a core

Assembly and Fine-Tuning

  • Assembled all components into the motor frame
  • Adjusted the position of the hall effect sensor to accurately control motor timing
  • Ensured correct interaction between magnets and electromagnet to achieve smooth rotation

Skills Demonstrated

  • Digital fabrication (laser cutting, 3D printing)
  • PCB soldering and electrical wiring
  • Metal shaping, cutting, and drilling
  • Understanding of basic motor operation and sensor alignment
  • Attention to detail and precision in assembly
