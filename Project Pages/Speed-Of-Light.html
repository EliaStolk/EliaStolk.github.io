Speed of Light Measurement

Experimental Measurement of the Speed of Light Using an Oscilloscope

Overview

In this project, an experiment to measure the speed of light was designed using lab equipment accessible to university students. The focus was on translating theoretical knowledge into practical measurement and understanding the limitations of instruments like oscilloscopes and function generators. This experiment was completed as a two-person lab team where my contribution was with experiemental design, oscilloscope setup, timing analysis, and report preparation

Methods and Tools

  • Function generator and laser: Produced a square wave to trigger the laser with a clear start and stop signal
  • Photodiode with amplifier: Detected the laser signal at different distances to measure the time delay
  • Oscilloscope: Measured the voltage signals to determine the propagation time of light
  • Data analysis in MATLAB: Processed the measurements, calculated differences in distance and time, and fitted lines to estimate the speed of light
  • Report writing: LaTeX based technical report following standard scientific structure and formatting

Key points:

  • Took multiple measurements at distances from 0.6m to 2.1m
  • Corrected for equipment delay by comparing time differences between distances
  • Selected clear reference points on the oscilloscope to minimize measurement error

Results and Observations

  • Measured values across three datasets averaged to (2.7 ± 8.1) × 10^8 m/s
  • High uncertainty was observed due to limitations in measuring nanosecond-scale signals, small sample sizes, and human error in identifying reference points
  • Excluding the first dataset improved the result to (3.1 ± 2.2) × 10^8 m/s, which aligns better with the accepted value of 2.998 × 10^8 m/s

Skills Demonstrated

  • Translating theoretical physics into experimental procedures
  • Use of oscilloscopes, lasers, function generators, and photodiodes
  • Data analysis and error estimation
  • Understanding instrument limitations and experimental uncertainty
  • Attention to detail and troubleshooting in practical measurements

What I Learned

  • Practical challenges in measuring extremely fast signals
  • Importance of clear reference points and repeatable methods
  • How small uncertainties can propagate and impact final results
  • How to use MATLAB to process and analyze experimental data
